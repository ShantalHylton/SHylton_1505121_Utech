AI in Education — My Report

Based on a review of the Top 20 Profitable AI Business Ideas blog by ClickUp, I have chosen to reflect on AI in Education as the most exciting and personally relevant idea from the list.

What is AI in Education?

AI in Education refers to the use of artificial intelligence technologies to personalise, enhance, and scale the learning experience. This includes intelligent tutoring systems, adaptive learning platforms, automated grading tools, AI-powered content creation, and virtual learning assistants available around the clock.

How it Can Be Applied in My World

As a student at UTech studying Data Analytics, AI in Education is directly applicable to my world. An AI-powered learning assistant could help me understand complex statistical concepts, provide personalised practice problems based on my weak areas, and give real-time feedback on my code or data models. At an institutional level, UTech could leverage AI to identify at-risk students early and allow lecturers to intervene before it is too late.

Why It Would Be Useful

AI in Education democratises access to quality learning. Not every student can afford private tutors. With AI tools, any student with an internet connection can receive personalised guidance at any time. For a country like Jamaica, where educational resources can sometimes be limited, this technology has the potential to level the playing field.

Ethical Issues

  1. Academic Dishonesty — Students may use AI to complete assignments rather than as a learning aid.
  2. Data Privacy — AI systems collect vast amounts of student data that must be carefully protected.
  3. Bias in Algorithms — AI trained on biased datasets may disadvantage certain student groups.
  4. Over-dependence on Technology — Heavy reliance on AI could reduce critical thinking skills.

My Solutions

  • Institutions should establish clear AI usage policies for academic work.
  • Student data should be governed by strict privacy policies with full transparency.
  • AI vendors should regularly audit algorithms for bias across all student demographics.
  • AI should supplement human teaching, not replace it.

Example App in This Area

Khan Academy’s Khanmigo — an AI-powered tutor built on GPT-4 that guides students through problems using the Socratic method. Visit: www.khanacademy.org/khan-labs

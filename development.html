Reflections on Twelve Pillars by Jim Rohn & Chris Widener

Twelve Pillars — Chapter Summaries (Chapters 4–7)

Twelve Pillars by Jim Rohn and Chris Widener is a powerful story about a young man named Michael Jones who, after a car breakdown, encounters an elderly caretaker named Charlie who teaches him twelve life-changing principles through conversations on the estate of a deceased business legend. Chapters 4 through 7 each deliver a profound pillar that I have chosen to align meaningfully with my own life and journey.

Chapter 4 — Pillar Four: Attitude

Summary: In this chapter, Charlie teaches Michael that attitude is a choice, arguably the most important choice a person makes every single day. He explains that life is ten percent what happens to you and ninety percent how you respond to it. Your attitude determines the altitude you reach. A positive growth-oriented attitude attracts opportunity and propels you forward, while a negative attitude keeps you stuck regardless of your circumstances.

My Reflection: This chapter resonates deeply with me because I have personally experienced the power of attitude in my academic life. There have been moments at UTech where subjects like Data Analysis and Database Design have been extremely challenging. In those moments I had a choice to give up and complain, or to adjust my attitude and push through with determination. Every time I chose the latter, I came out stronger. Charlie's lesson reminded me that my attitude is the one thing no circumstance can take from me. My mantra — “I can do all things through Christ who strengthens me” — is itself an attitude. It is a daily decision to believe that I am capable, equipped, and destined for greatness.

Chapter 5 — Pillar Five: Living with Purpose

Summary: Charlie tells Michael that without a clear purpose, life becomes a series of random events with no meaningful direction. He emphasises that purpose gives your life a North Star — it answers the question “why am I doing this?” Purpose does not have to be a grand mission statement; it can be as personal and specific as a commitment to your family, your community, or your craft. People who live with purpose are more focused, more motivated, and more resilient when obstacles arise.

My Reflection: This chapter challenged me to think deeply about why I am studying Computer Information Systems. My purpose goes beyond earning a degree. I want to use data and technology to help Caribbean organizations make better decisions and operate more efficiently. I want to build something that lasts. When I am sitting in a lecture after work or debugging code at midnight, it is my purpose that keeps me going. This chapter reinforced that I must hold tightly to that purpose especially on the hard days, because those are the days that define whether you truly meant what you said about your goals.

Chapter 6 — Pillar Six: Relationships

Summary: Charlie explains to Michael that the people you surround yourself with either lift you higher or pull you lower. He teaches that relationships are investments and you must choose them wisely and nurture them intentionally. Great relationships are built on trust, mutual respect, and a genuine desire for each other’s success. He also warns that some relationships, no matter how familiar, may need to be released if they consistently drain your energy, lower your standards, or derail your progress.

My Reflection: This pillar has been one of the most practically relevant lessons in my life. At university, the people I choose to study with, collaborate with, and spend time with have a direct impact on my grades, my mindset, and my motivation. Being a mentor in the UTech Mentorship Programme has also made me more intentional about relationships. I recognise that I have a responsibility to pour into others just as others have poured into me. This chapter also reminded me of my faith. My relationship with God is the most important one I maintain, and every other relationship must align with the values that relationship is built on — integrity, purpose, love, and growth.

Chapter 7 — Pillar Seven: Being a Lifelong Learner

Summary: In this chapter, Charlie challenges Michael to commit to never-ending personal and professional growth. He argues that formal education ends but learning never should. Reading books, seeking mentors, attending seminars, learning from failures, and staying curious are the hallmarks of people who continue to grow long after their degrees are framed on the wall. Charlie states that the day you stop learning is the day you start declining.

My Reflection: As someone studying Data Analytics and Technology Entrepreneurship, this pillar speaks directly to my industry. Technology evolves at a breathtaking pace. The tools and programming languages that are relevant today may be outdated in five years. If I stop learning after graduating, I will quickly become irrelevant in the field I am so passionate about. This chapter has committed me to a life of continuous learning, whether through certifications like my pending Google Cloud Data Analytics credential, through reading industry publications, or through building real projects that stretch my skills. Lifelong learning is not just a strategy for my career — it is a way of life that keeps me humble, hungry, and relevant.
